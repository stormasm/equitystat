Zrato Tutorial

Last modified on Wednesday June 29, 2005

Before running the programs in this tutorial be sure you have read the installation document. Assuming Zrato is properly installed and the simple test at the end of the installation document has run successfully, then you are now ready to run the tutorial.

It is also important to note that the data in the zrato database is only a sample for a particular industry group. Depending on your trading strategies and scenario, the database has to be populated on a regular basis with up to date financial information. Please contact us to find out what the best strategy is for your company for accessing fresh data. We provide consulting services on how to parse a data feed and store the information in the database.

Zrato can be broken down to 3 simple steps.

  • 1. Populate the database
  • 2. Run your trading algorithms
  • 3. View the output or results

In this tutorial, step 1 is already taken care of for you. By installing the zrato database in your MySql server you have populated your database with a sample set of data. In the future, you will need to populate your database with a specified set of companies that you are interested in following. This set of companies can also be based on an index or an industry group or both.

For now, we will assume that your database is populated.

The next step is to run your trading algorithms. We have designed a 200 day Moving Average calculation. This algorithm calculates the average of the past 200 days of trading for each company in the database each day over the total period of time in the hp (historical price) table. It then looks at the 200 day moving average on a particular day along with the closing price on that day and determines if the price has crossed over the 200 day moving average. If it has, it then logs the date in the ma table in which the cross over occurred along with the direction in which it crossed the average. In a bullish scenario, the price crosses the 200 day moving average in an upward direction and so up is logged. In a bearish scenario, the price on a particular day crosses the 200 day moving average in a downward direction and so down is logged. If nothing happened on that day, in other words no crossing occurred then nothing is logged. It is now time to run this algorithm and then review the ma table to see what your data looks like.

Tutorial Step One, calculate the moving averages and populate the ma table

  • ant ma

The final step is to review the output of the results of running your trading algorithm. Zrato does this by generating a report based on iText a powerful open source Java reporting tool that has the ability to deliver rich content onto the screen via PDF. The reporting engine is now ready to generate out a PDF report.

Tutorial Step Two, build the moving average report.

  • ant gm

The PDF report will end up located on your disk in the directory where you installed the Zrato product. If you look in that directory, you should see the file Matechg00.pdf

Take a look at the Matechg00.pdf report and note how the companies whose 50 and 200 day Moving averages have UP directions have been in a bullish trading pattern for quite some time. This means that on that particular day a bullish signal occurred, and the closing price crossed the 200 day moving average upward and its stayed above the moving average delineating the fact that the underlying fundamentals of this company are improving thus driving the stock price higher and higher over time. To confirm this, go to your favorite website with stock charts and look at several things. First of all look at the charts of these companies. Note, that for the past year their stock has been going up, you can also plot the stock price versus the 200 day moving average on many popular financial web sites.

  • 1. Populate the database
  • 2. Run your trading algorithms
  • 3. View the output or results

iText Reporting Tool

Last modified on Tuesday July 5, 2005

Before trying to dive into understanding iText you should go to the iText web site and review the documention provided with the distribution. I would even recommend downloading a copy of iText and running through some of the demos.

For those of you who are Java programmers and want to start working with iText the most important document to read on the iText web site is the Tutorial. This outlines in much detail the different objects and their attributes for each type of element found in a report or document.

All of the main iText reports for the Zrato system are found in the packages starting with com.arcadian.itext.*.

There are 3 classes of reports generated in Zrato.

  • Algorithmic reports namely Moving Average
  • Sector / Industry Reports Based on Market Capitalizations
  • Future (EPS) Earnings Per Share Reports

The report names correlate with the following types of reports

  • EpsTechg00.pdf -- Earnings Per Share Report for the Technology Sector
  • Techg00.pdf -- Technology Sector Report
  • Matechg00.pdf -- Moving Average Report for the Technology Sector

To generate out all of the reports at once use ant ga.

This is a good starting point for understanding what iText can do for you in concert with a Relational Database.
